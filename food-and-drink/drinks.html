Drinks

Bulgaria's beverages may be even more distinct than the foods. I was introduced to most of these drinks before going to Bulgaria, but they are still hard to find in the US, so be sure to try them while you're there!

Rakia

Practically the national drink, rakia is brandy made from fermented fruit. The most popular is probably grape, but I also saw plum, apricot, cherry, and apple. Its typical alcohol content is 40%, but it can be higher depending on the brand. Rakia is consumed in small quantities, usually either 50ml or 100ml, and is meant to accompany an appetizer, like a shopska salad (cucumbers, tomatoes, onions, peppers and white cheese) or cured meats.

Wine from the Thracian Valley

Grapes grow throughout the country, and local Bulgarian wines are easy to find and delicious. Not to be missed!

Ayran

A simple drink of yogurt thinned with water, ayran is immesely popular and goes well with banitsa. Speaking of yogurt, Bulgarian yogurt is world-class and world-famous.

Boza

My boyfriend had me try this, and it tasted unlike anything I have ever tried. However, all of the Bulgarians I know grew up drinking it and love it. It's probably an acquired taste, but you should buy a bottle and give it a try, just to get the full experience! While it's mostly marketed to children, the drink is actually slightly alcoholic - but only about 0.5%. It's made from boiled fermented wheat, with some sugar added.
